The convex hull of a shape is the original shape together with the minimum additional area needed to make it convex. To visualize the convex hull, imagine placing a rubber band tightly around the perimeter of the shape. If the shape is a square, or a rectangle, the rubber band will fit exactly along the edges of the shape. If the shape is not rectangular, the rubber band will stretch across any gaps created between corners of the shape. This region enclosed by the rubber band is the convex hull.
