Compactness in the Courts: Pennsylvania's 7th congressional district adopted in 2011 (shown below) has been widely cited as an example of a district that is far from compact as a result of gerrymandering. In 2018, the state's Supreme Court ruled this (and the rest of the district plan) to be an unconstitutional gerrymander, and required the state to create a new map. [1.12.5]
