The good news is that mathematics provides tools to measure various forms of “fairness” in addition to ways to detect both forms of gerrymandering. We begin in Section 1.5 by introducing compactness formulas to quantify the geometry of districts. Then in Section 1.6, we use a couple of different mathematical approaches to investigate partisan gerrymandering. Next, in Section 1.7, we unpack the ways statistics can be used to unveil racial gerrymandering. Finally, in Section 1.8 we introduce the concept of outlier analysis as a way to identify unfair maps.
